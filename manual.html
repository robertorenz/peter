Peter & the Wolf

The Player's Manual — a six-part meadow adventure after Prokofiev
▶ Play the game

📖The tale

You are Peter, out in the great green meadow beyond Grandfather's gate. The little bird is your scout, the duck paddles in the pond — and somewhere between the trees prowls the wolf, hunting with its eyes and ears. Six little adventures retell the story: gather the orchard's apples, rescue your friends, walk Grandpa home through the dark, and at last snare the wolf by the tail beneath the old oak, just as in the tale.

On touch screens: drag anywhere on the left half of the screen to walk (a little joystick appears under your finger), and tap the right half to act. Two buttons sit on the right edge: 🤫 tip-toe toggles quiet walking on and off (it glows gold while active), and ♪ whistle calls a friend — just like Shift and E.

🗺️The six adventures

1

The Meadow

The apples wear number badges and must be gathered in order, 1 to last — the next one in the sequence glows gold (and shines gold on the minimap). Gather them all, then slip home through the gate… or lure the wolf under the rope by the old oak and press Space for the hero's ending. Watch the wolf's suspicion meter — running, twig-snaps and whistles all feed it.

2

Save the Bird

The cat is stalking the little bird. Freeze the cat with thrown apples (Space) and walk the bird to the tall tree in the far corner. Whistle (E) to call the bird to you from across the meadow.

3

Rescue the Duck

Lead the duck across the wide meadow to the pond. She follows close behind you — stun the wolf with apples and weave through the rocks and cliffs for cover.

4

Trap the Wolf

Find the rope, carry it to the old oak (Space throws it over the branch), then lure the wolf beneath the dangling snare and spring it. Carrying the rope slows you down — drop it with Q if the wolf closes in.

5

Grandfather's Gate

Night has fallen. The fireflies light only a small circle around Peter, and the wolf hunts whichever of you it can see. Keep Grandpa close — he cannot run — and walk him home to the gate. Bushes hide you; apples drive the wolf off.

6

The Great Chase

Run, Peter, run! One long sprint across a meadow four screens wide, the wolf at your heels, day fading to night as you go — ending at the old oak's waiting snare. After the capture: the Triumphal Procession, and the curtain call.

Finish the tale and the title page unlocks chapter select plus Endless Dusk — survive a gathering wolf pack for points. The tale then begins anew, a little wilder each cycle: Storybook, Wild (autumn), Prokofiev (winter).

🍎Apples on the Meadow — the full rules

  • Gather in order. Only the apple whose number matches the HUD's next # can be picked up; the wrong apple just reminds you gently.
  • Throwing undoes gathering. Space hurls an apple at the wolf — but apples leave the basket in reverse order: the last one you gathered flies first.
  • Everything bounces. A hit stuns the wolf and the apple caroms off his flank along the line of your throw; a miss ricochets off trees, rocks and the meadow's edge, losing speed with each bounce.
  • Fetch it back. Wherever the apple rolls to rest, it lies waiting with its number — and rejoins the sequence, so it must be re-gathered in order before the harvest is complete. (No double points for re-picking!)
  • The snare minds the count. The oak's rope only arms while you hold every apple — throw one and it lets back down until the basket is full again.

🐺Knowing the wolf

  • Patrol — it wanders calmly. Hunt — if it sees you in the open or hears you running close by, its eyes glow red and it gives chase.
  • When it gets close it crouches with a flashing ❗, then pounces in a straight line — sidestep the telegraph.
  • You have three hearts per chapter; a bite costs one, with a knock-back and a moment of safety.
  • It fears the hunters: when their patrol wanders near (a far-off horn sounds first), the wolf slinks away, tail tucked — a free moment to breathe or set your trap.
  • It learns: hide in the same bush too long while it hunts and it sniffs the spot out for good.

🤫The stealth toolkit

  • Tip-toe (Shift) — silent movement when the wolf is near.
  • Trees & rocks — break line of sight; but sprinting close past one can snap a twig every predator turns toward.
  • Berry bushes — step inside and you vanish from the wolf's senses.
  • The scout bird — on chapter 1 it wheels above the wolf wherever it prowls, so you always know where danger is; whistle to send it diving at the wolf.
  • Shakeable trees — out of ammo on the rescue chapters? Space at a tree shakes an apple or two loose… noisily.
💡 Tip: apples, speed and hearts kept all earn points; every chapter ends with a ★-rating and your best total is saved. Your run — chapter, tier and score — is saved too, so you can close the browser and pick the tale back up.
⚠️ Mind the meter: on the Meadow, the wolf's suspicion bar fills with every noise you make. Full bar means the hunt is on — go quiet, break its line of sight, and let it cool.

🎵Music & the narrator

Every chapter plays its character's actual Prokofiev leitmotif — Peter's strings, the duck's oboe, the wolf's horns — from a tiny in-code synth (no audio files at all). Between chapters, storybook interludes quote the tale; tick "read the story aloud" on the title page and a narrator speaks them. M mutes everything.
